Sentiment Analysis of Greek Plays

This visualization entry is going to be a little different than what I typically post. Usually, I write code that creates stand alone plots, along with a step by step tutorial. This time I decided to create what amounts to a poster. It may not be shocking to hear that ggplot isn't the best tool for graphic designs, even if it is wonderful for graphics.

I would even go as far as to say that this poster is as much about the text as it is about the plots. I first tried to use canva to create the poster but found it to be quite irritating to use. It service uses a freemium model and seems to purposefully handicap the free version. For example, you can't resize the poster. If you want to resize you have to start over, which needless to say, sucks.

To create the final poster, I exported three images from R and pasted them into keynote and then did all the arranging and text work from there.

As far as how the plot was created, you can find a detailed tutorial as part of an upcoming udemy course I am writing on NLP, or if you don't want to pay for that, check out my github.

Without further ado, here's the poster!
